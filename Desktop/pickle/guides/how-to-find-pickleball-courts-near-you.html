How to find pickleball courts near you

Where to actually play

You have a paddle and shoes. Now, where do you go? Finding courts is easier than it looks. Here are the ways that actually work.

Use a court finder app

Start with a court finder like Pickleheads or Places2Play. Type in your area and they show nearby courts, often with open play times and skill levels. This is the fastest way to see what is close to you.

Check Facebook groups

Almost every town has a local pickleball Facebook group. Search your city plus "pickleball." These groups post open play times, beginner meetups, and last-minute games. They are also where you ask questions and meet people.

Try parks and rec

Your city parks and recreation department often runs public courts and beginner sessions, sometimes free or very cheap. Check their website or call. Community centers and the YMCA frequently have indoor courts too, which is handy when it is hot or raining.

Just ask

Once you play once, ask the people there where else they play and when. Pickleball players know every court in a 20 minute radius, and they love sharing. One session usually unlocks three new places to go.

Look for the magic word

When you scan times, look for "beginner" or "all levels." Those sessions are friendlier and slower, and nobody expects you to be good. Save the "advanced" and "3.5+" sessions for later. More on walking in alone: how to join open play.

Pick one spot with a beginner session this week. That is the only step that matters right now.
