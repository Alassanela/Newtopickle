Where to start with pickleball

Six steps, in the right order

If you just found pickleball and have no idea where to begin, start here. This page gives you a simple path from zero to ready. Follow the steps in order. Each one builds on the last.

You do not need to read everything on this site. Just these six things first.

Step 1 — Get your gear

Before anything else, you need a paddle and the right shoes. You do not need to spend much. A solid first setup costs under $120, and a lot of that is optional. The shoes are not optional. Running shoes can roll your ankle on a pickleball court.

→ What you need to start playing pickleball

→ How to choose your first paddle

→ Court shoes vs running shoes

Step 2 — Learn the rules

The rules look complicated written down. They are not. You can learn enough to play in about five minutes of reading. Focus on three things: the serve, the two-bounce rule, and the kitchen. Everything else you will pick up on the court.

→ Pickleball rules for beginners (the simple version)

→ What is the kitchen?

→ How to serve

Step 3 — Understand scoring

Scoring trips up almost every beginner. The three-number system sounds confusing until someone explains the logic. Once it clicks, you will never forget it.

→ How to keep score without getting confused

Step 4 — Find a court

Courts are everywhere. You probably have beginner-friendly open play within a few miles of your house. Here is how to find it fast.

→ How to find pickleball courts near you

Step 5 — Show up

This is the hard one for most people. Walking up to a court full of strangers, alone, for the first time feels awkward. It stops being awkward after your first game. Here is exactly what to do and say.

Step 6 — Learn from your mistakes

Every beginner makes the same ten mistakes. Knowing what they are before you play means you will avoid most of them. And when you do make them, you will know how to fix them.

→ How to get better as a beginner

That is it. Six steps. You now know more than most people who walk onto a court for the first time. Go play.
